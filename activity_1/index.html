Food Pyramid vs Plate Ratio

These are the food groups for the food pyramid, 1 being the what we should consume the most, 5 being what we should consume the least.

  1. Grains: 6-11 servings
  2. Fruit: 2-4 servings
  3. Vegetables: 3-5 servings
  4. Meat, Poultry, Fish, Dry Beans, Eggs & Nuts: 2-3 servings
  5. Milk, Yogurt, & Cheese: 2-3 servings
  6. Fats, Oils, Sweets: use sparingly

These are the food groups for plate ratio.

  • Fruits:
  • Vegetables
  • Grains
  • Protein
  • Dairy

The food groups now have transitioned from pyramid style to plate ratio style. While the idea has stayed the same, the plate ratio style has broken things down into larger categories,
instead of multiple blocks to build a pyramid. Harvard's School of Public Health says:

"Vegetables, fruits, whole grains, healthy oils, and healthy proteins like nuts, beans,
fish, and chicken should make it into the shopping cart every week, along with a little yogurt
or other dairy foods if desired."
